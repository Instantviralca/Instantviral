# InstantViral v2

# Document 15.06 — Related Articles Engine

Version: 1.0
Status: Production Specification

## Purpose

Create a centralized Related Articles Engine for the Learn Center.

The engine should automatically select useful, relevant published articles for:

- Article pages
- Category pages
- Learn homepage
- Future author pages
- Future platform hubs
- Related service sections

The system must improve discovery and internal linking without showing drafts, duplicate cards, or unrelated content.

---

## Objectives

The engine should:

- Recommend genuinely relevant articles
- Prioritize the same topic cluster
- Use category, tag, platform, and intent signals
- Exclude the current article
- Exclude drafts and inactive articles
- Avoid repeated recommendations
- Support manual editorial overrides
- Support future popularity and behavioural signals
- Integrate with the Internal Linking Engine
- Remain deterministic and testable

---

## Supported Surfaces

### Article Pages

Display 3–4 related published articles.

### Learn Homepage

Support:

- Featured articles
- Recently published
- Platform-specific groups
- Editor-selected articles

### Category Pages

Support related or recommended articles from the same category.

### Author Pages

Support more articles by the same author.

### Future Platform Hubs

Support relevant guides for Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, and YouTube.

---

## Related Article Signals

Use the following signals:

1. Exact topic cluster
2. Shared primary category
3. Shared platform
4. Shared tags
5. Shared search intent
6. Related service connection
7. Same audience
8. Freshness
9. Editorial priority
10. Author relationship where appropriate

Do not use future popularity unless genuine analytics data exists.

---

Extend or use article data fields such as:

- relatedArticleSlugs

- keywords

- featured

## Manual Editorial Overrides

Support explicit manual relationships.

If `relatedArticleSlugs` is configured:

1. Validate each slug
2. Include valid published articles first
3. Exclude the current article
4. Exclude duplicates
5. Fill remaining slots automatically

Manual relationships should not bypass publication or safety rules.

---

## Automatic Scoring

Recommended scoring model:

- Same topic cluster: +10
- Same category: +7
- Same platform: +6
- Shared primary tag: +5
- Each additional shared tag: +2
- Same search intent: +4
- Shared related service: +3
- Same audience: +2
- Featured editorial status: +1
- Meaningfully updated recently: +1

Penalties:

- Current article: exclude
- Draft or unpublished: exclude
- Inactive article: exclude
- Duplicate slug: exclude
- Already displayed elsewhere in the same block: exclude
- Weak or no relevance: exclude
- Very old and outdated content: penalty when freshness matters

Weights should remain configurable.

## Result Limits

Recommended:

- Article page: 3–4
- Category page recommendation block: 3–6
- Author page: 3–6
- Homepage group: 3–6

Do not render excessive related-content grids.

---

## Diversity Rules

Avoid showing four nearly identical articles.

Recommended diversity:

- Maximum 2 articles with the exact same narrow subtopic
- Prefer varied but complementary intent
- Avoid repeating the same article in adjacent sections
- Prefer at least one practical guide where available

---

## Freshness Rules

Freshness should be a secondary signal.

Do not prefer a newer but irrelevant article over a highly relevant one.

Use:

- publishedAt
- updatedAt
- evergreen status
- content freshness status when added

Do not fake freshness by changing dates automatically.

---

## Related Service Coordination

Articles connected to the same service may receive a relevance boost.

Examples:

- An Instagram follower growth guide may relate to Buy Instagram Followers
- A YouTube video promotion guide may relate to Buy YouTube Views

Do not turn the related article engine into a sales-only recommendation system.

## Eligibility Rules

An article is eligible only when:

- Published is true
- Active is true
- Public route exists
- Canonical metadata exists
- It is not the current article
- It is not already selected
- It is not blocked from recommendations
- It has sufficient relevance

Drafts, previews, scheduled articles, and archived content must not appear publicly.

## Empty State

Public article page:

Hide the Related Articles section when no relevant published articles exist.

Admin/editor preview:

Show why no related articles were found.

Do not generate placeholder recommendations.

---

## Internal Linking

Every related article card should use:

- Canonical article route
- Descriptive article title
- Category context
- Shared Internal Linking Engine

Do not hardcode URLs.

---

## SEO Rules

Related article links are normal crawlable internal links.

Requirements:

- Link only to canonical published pages
- Do not add `nofollow`
- Do not link to search/filter URLs
- Do not link to preview or draft routes
- Avoid excessive repeated exact-match anchors
- Do not create hidden SEO-only links

## Admin and Editorial Preview

Prepare future editor tools for:

- Preview automatic recommendations
- View score explanation
- Add manual related articles
- Reorder manual articles
- Remove a recommendation
- Mark article as excluded from recommendations
- View invalid or missing slugs
- Detect circular editorial patterns
- View insufficient relevance warnings

Do not implement backend CRUD yet.

---

## Validation

Detect:

- Missing related article slug
- Draft article selected manually
- Duplicate related slug
- Current article linked to itself
- Article missing canonical route
- Unpublished article recommended
- Irrelevant low-score relationship
- Same recommendations repeated excessively
- Broken article route

Report issues rather than silently fabricating replacements.

---

## Accessibility

- Semantic section heading
- Descriptive card links
- Keyboard accessible
- Visible focus states
- Article images have alt text
- Dates use semantic time elements
- No inaccessible carousel required
- Prefer grid or stacked layout
- 44px minimum touch targets

---

## Responsive Rules

Desktop:

- 3 or 4-card grid depending on container width

Tablet:

- 2-column grid

Mobile:

- Single-column stack
- No forced horizontal carousel
- No clipped titles or excerpts

---

## Performance

- Score server-side where possible
- Cache recommendations for published content
- Recalculate when article relationships change
- Limit candidate data to safe article metadata
- Avoid sending full article bodies to the client
- Lazy-load below-fold images
- Avoid expensive scoring during every client render

---

## Security

- Never expose draft metadata
- Never expose private author information
- Validate all article slugs
- Sanitize visible excerpts
- Do not trust manually entered routes without registry validation
