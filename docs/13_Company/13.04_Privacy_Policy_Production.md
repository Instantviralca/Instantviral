# Document 13.04 — Privacy Policy Production Specification

Version: 1.0  
Status: Legal Template Requiring Professional Review  
Public URL: /privacy-policy  
Jurisdiction Focus: Canada

> Important: This document is a business-ready drafting template, not legal advice. Before publication, it must be reviewed against InstantViral’s actual data practices, payment providers, analytics tools, hosting arrangements, business location, and the privacy laws that apply to its customers.

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the production Privacy Policy page for InstantViral.

The policy should explain, in clear language:

- What personal information InstantViral collects
- Why that information is collected
- How it is used and disclosed
- Which service providers may process it
- How long it is retained
- How it is protected
- Which privacy choices and rights customers have
- How customers can contact the privacy lead
- How privacy incidents and policy changes are handled

The final implementation must reflect the real website and business operations. Do not list tools, providers, cookies, payment processors, addresses, phone numbers, retention periods, or international transfers unless they are accurate.

---

# 2. Canadian Privacy Foundation

The policy should be drafted to support compliance with applicable Canadian private-sector privacy requirements, including the principles of accountability, identified purposes, consent, limited collection, limited use and retention, accuracy, safeguards, openness, individual access, and complaint handling.

InstantViral should identify a person responsible for privacy compliance and provide a working contact method.

If InstantViral conducts business in Québec or handles personal information covered by Québec private-sector privacy law, the final policy and operational practices should also be reviewed for Québec-specific requirements.

# 7. Scope

This policy applies to personal information handled through:

- InstantViral.ca
- Service and pricing pages
- Cart and checkout
- Order configuration
- Order confirmation
- Order tracking
- Contact and support forms
- Email communications
- Administrative order processing
- Analytics and security tools that are actually enabled

This policy does not automatically apply to independent third-party websites, social media platforms, or payment providers that publish their own privacy policies.

Only include categories InstantViral actually collects.

## Information Customers Provide

Possible categories:

- Full name
- Email address
- Order ID
- Customer support messages
- Optional order notes
- Coupon code
- Selected service and package
- Public social media username
- Public profile, page, post, Reel, channel, or video URL
- Custom comment text where a supported package requires it

## Transaction and Order Information

Possible categories:

- Price and currency

# 9. How Information Is Collected

Information may be collected:

- Directly from forms completed by the customer
- During cart and checkout
- Through order tracking lookups
- Through customer support communications
- Automatically through cookies, logs, analytics, and security technologies that are actually enabled
- From payment and infrastructure providers where necessary to confirm transactions, prevent fraud, or operate the service

Do not claim collection sources that do not exist.

---

# 10. Purposes for Collection and Use

InstantViral may use personal information for the following real and necessary purposes:

- Providing requested services
- Processing and confirming orders
- Associating selected packages with the correct public social media destination
- Communicating order updates
- Providing order tracking
- Responding to customer questions
- Processing payments through enabled payment providers
- Applying valid coupons and calculating totals
- Preventing duplicate, fraudulent, abusive, or unauthorized activity
- Troubleshooting technical issues
- Maintaining security logs
- Improving website usability and performance
- Meeting legal, accounting, tax, dispute-resolution, and recordkeeping obligations
- Sending marketing communications only where legally permitted and consented to

Do not use personal information for materially new purposes without assessing whether new consent or notice is required.

---

# 11. Consent and Privacy Choices

InstantViral should obtain meaningful consent appropriate to the sensitivity of the information and the purpose of collection.

Customers should be able to:

- Decide whether to receive optional marketing emails
- Manage non-essential cookies where a consent tool is required or implemented
- Withdraw consent for optional uses, subject to legal and contractual restrictions
- Request information about privacy practices
- Request access to or correction of personal information

Withdrawing consent may affect services that cannot be provided without the required information.

---

# 12. How We Share Information

InstantViral should disclose personal information only as necessary.

Possible recipients, when actually used:

- Payment processors
- Hosting and cloud infrastructure providers
- Email delivery providers
- Analytics providers
- Fraud-prevention and security providers
- Customer support tools
- Professional advisers
- Government, regulators, courts, or law enforcement where legally required
- A buyer, investor, or successor during a genuine business transaction, subject to appropriate safeguards

InstantViral should not sell personal information unless its real business practices change and the policy and consent process are updated accordingly.

Do not publish provider names unless they are confirmed.

---

# 13. Payment Information

InstantViral should not store complete payment-card details unless its actual payment architecture is designed and certified to do so.

Payment information may be collected and processed directly by the enabled payment provider.

The final policy should identify:

- The payment providers actually available
- Whether InstantViral receives payment tokens, transaction identifiers, payer email, billing details, or payment status
- What information is retained for accounting, fraud prevention, refunds, and dispute management

Never state that a payment provider is used until it is actually integrated.

---

Possible categories:

- Cookie preferences

## Analytics Technologies

---

# 15. International Processing and Service Providers

Some service providers may process information outside the customer’s province or country.

Before publication, InstantViral must verify:

- Where hosting occurs
- Where email delivery occurs
- Where payment data is processed
- Where analytics data is processed
- Whether administrative access occurs from another country
- Which contractual and security safeguards are used

The production policy should explain that information processed in another jurisdiction may be subject to that jurisdiction’s laws.

Do not make a “Canada-only storage” claim unless verified.

---

# 16. Retention

InstantViral should retain personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the identified purposes and applicable legal obligations.

Create and document real retention periods for:

- Unfinished cart data
- Contact form enquiries
- Customer support records
- Order and transaction records
- Payment confirmation records
- Order configuration data
- Order tracking data
- Marketing consent records
- Analytics data
- Security logs
- Internal administrative notes

When information is no longer needed, it should be securely deleted, anonymized, or otherwise disposed of according to the retention schedule.

Do not publish invented retention periods.

---

# 17. Security Safeguards

InstantViral should use safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity and volume of personal information.

Possible safeguards include:

- HTTPS
- Access controls
- Strong administrator authentication
- Role restrictions when additional staff are added
- Secure password storage
- Encryption where appropriate
- Server-side payment verification
- Protected environment variables
- Audit logs
- Backups
- Rate limiting
- Monitoring
- Vendor due diligence
- Staff confidentiality practices
- Secure deletion

No website can guarantee absolute security. Do not claim that information is “100% secure.”

---

# 18. Privacy Incidents

InstantViral should maintain a privacy incident process that includes:

- Containing the incident
- Investigating what happened
- Assessing the sensitivity and possible harm
- Recording relevant facts and decisions
- Notifying affected individuals and regulators where legally required
- Correcting weaknesses
- Preserving required breach records

The public policy should provide a contact method for reporting suspected privacy or security concerns.

---

# 19. Access and Correction

Subject to applicable law and valid exceptions, individuals may request:

- Access to personal information held about them
- Information about how it has been used or disclosed
- Correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
- Withdrawal of consent for optional uses
- Information about the privacy complaint process

InstantViral should verify identity before responding.

Requests should be submitted through the verified privacy contact listed in the final policy.

Do not promise a response timeframe unless the business can meet the legal and operational requirement stated.

---

# 20. Marketing Communications

Marketing email should be sent only when legally permitted.

Each marketing message should include:

- Accurate sender identification
- A valid contact method
- A functioning unsubscribe mechanism

Transactional order emails should remain separate from optional promotional consent where appropriate.

The final implementation must honour unsubscribe requests and suppression records.

---

# 21. Children’s Privacy

InstantViral services are not directed to children below the minimum age permitted by applicable law and platform requirements.

The final policy should define the age threshold after legal review and alignment with:

- Applicable privacy law
- Payment-provider requirements
- Instagram terms
- TikTok terms
- Facebook terms
- YouTube terms

Do not select an arbitrary age without review.

If InstantViral learns that it collected personal information from a child without valid authorization, it should take appropriate steps to delete or otherwise address the information.

---

# 22. Third-Party Links and Social Platforms

InstantViral pages may link to:

- Instagram
- TikTok
- Facebook
- YouTube
- Payment providers
- Other external websites

Those third parties control their own privacy practices.

InstantViral should not claim responsibility for independent third-party privacy policies, content, or security.

Customers should review the relevant third-party policies before providing information directly to those services.

---

# 23. Changes to this Policy

InstantViral may update the Privacy Policy when:

- Data practices change
- New tools or providers are introduced
- Legal requirements change
- New products or features launch
- Business operations change

The revised policy should display a new “Last Updated” date.

For material changes, InstantViral should consider a more prominent notice or renewed consent where required.

---

# 24. Contact and Complaints

The final policy must identify the real privacy contact.

Required fields:

- Privacy contact or responsible person
- Business/legal name
- Working email address
- Mailing address when legally or operationally required
- Complaint escalation process

Use configuration values only.

Do not invent contact details.

Suggested wording:

If you have a question, access request, correction request, or complaint about InstantViral’s privacy practices, contact the person responsible for privacy using the details listed below. We will review the request, verify identity where necessary, and respond according to applicable law and our internal privacy procedures.
