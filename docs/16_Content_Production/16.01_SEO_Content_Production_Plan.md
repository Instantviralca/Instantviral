# Document 16.01 — SEO Content Production Plan

Version: 1.0
Status: Production Specification

## Purpose

Define the production plan for creating real Learn Center articles that support organic search growth, topical authority, and service-page discovery.

This document controls:

- Topic selection
- Search intent
- Content clusters
- Publishing priorities
- Internal linking
- Article quality
- Editorial workflow
- Service-page support
- Content maintenance

Do not publish placeholder, thin, copied, or mass-generated articles.

---

# 1. Content Goals

The Learn Center should:

- Answer genuine user questions
- Support the 12 approved service pages
- Build topical authority for Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, YouTube, and social media marketing
- Help creators, businesses, agencies, and personal brands
- Attract informational search traffic
- Convert relevant readers without aggressive selling
- Create strong internal-link pathways
- Remain accurate and useful after publication

# 5. Topic Selection Criteria

Each topic must meet most of these conditions:

- Clear search intent
- Relevant to the supported platforms
- Useful to the target audience
- Can provide a complete answer
- Fits an existing topic cluster
- Has a logical internal-link destination
- Does not require unsupported claims
- Is not substantially duplicated by another article
- Can be maintained over time
- Supports business goals without becoming thin commercial copy

---

# 6. Search Intent Types

Classify every article as one primary intent:

- Informational
- How-to
- Beginner Guide
- Strategy
- Troubleshooting
- Comparison
- Commercial Investigation
- Platform Update

Do not combine multiple conflicting intents unnecessarily.

# 14. Content Quality Standard

Every article should:

- Answer the main question early
- Use a clear structure
- Provide practical guidance
- Include original analysis where possible
- Use plain English
- Use short paragraphs
- Avoid filler
- Avoid repetitive headings
- Avoid keyword stuffing
- Avoid exaggerated claims
- Distinguish facts from recommendations
- Cite trustworthy sources where needed
- Include useful examples
- End with actionable takeaways

---

# 15. Article Length

Use topic-appropriate depth.

Typical ranges:

- Short focused article: 900–1,300 words
- Standard guide: 1,300–2,000 words
- Comprehensive guide: 2,000–3,000 words

Do not pad articles to reach a word count.

Quality and completeness are more important than length.

---

# 16. Internal Linking Rules

Each article should link to:

- Its category page
- 2–5 relevant published articles when available
- 1–2 relevant service pages where appropriate
- FAQ, Contact, or legal pages when context requires
- Official external sources where factual support is needed

Do not force a service link into an unrelated article.

---

# 17. CTA Rules

Use subtle, relevant CTAs.

Examples:

- Explore Instagram Followers Packages
- Compare TikTok Views Options
- View YouTube Subscriber Packages
- Contact Support

Maximum:

- One prominent service CTA in the article body
- One final CTA near the end

Do not use aggressive or misleading language.

---

# 18. Source and Research Rules

Use trustworthy sources for:

- Platform rules
- Product features
- Algorithm explanations
- Privacy and safety topics
- Legal or policy references
- Statistics
- Industry research

Priority:

1. Official platform documentation
2. Primary research
3. Reputable industry publications
4. Carefully selected secondary analysis

Do not invent statistics or quote unsupported claims.

---

# 19. Originality Rules

Articles must not:

- Copy competitors
- Closely paraphrase a single source
- Reuse the same introduction across articles
- Duplicate sections across platform articles without meaningful adaptation
- Use generic AI filler
- Publish fake examples
- Present unverified opinions as facts

---

# 20. Platform Policy Safety

Content must not advise readers to:

- Share passwords
- Circumvent security
- Mislead users
- Violate laws
- Use fake identities
- Manipulate payment systems
- Bypass platform restrictions

When discussing growth services, use transparent and conditional wording.

---

# 21. Article Images

Each article should support:

- One featured image
- Optional supporting illustrations
- Optional screenshots when permitted
- Optional charts where useful

Required:

- Correct dimensions
- Alt text
- Captions where helpful
- Rights verification
- Optimized WebP or AVIF
- No unauthorized hotlinking

---

# 22. Author Assignment

Every article must have a real active author.

Do not publish with:

- Fake author names
- Invented credentials
- Generic unsupported expert titles
- Inactive author records

---

# 23. Editorial Status

Use:

- Planned
- Brief Ready
- Draft
- In Review
- Approved
- Scheduled
- Published
- Updating
- Archived

Only Published articles are public.

---

# 24. Content Calendar

Maintain:

- Article
- Platform
- Cluster
- Priority
- Author
- Draft due date
- Review date
- Publication date
- Update date
- Status

Avoid publishing several nearly identical topics on the same day.

---

# 25. Update Schedule

Review articles based on topic sensitivity.

Suggested:

- Platform algorithm or feature articles: every 3–6 months
- General evergreen guides: every 6–12 months
- Policy-sensitive content: when platform rules change
- Statistics-heavy content: when source data updates

Do not change the modified date without a meaningful update.

---

# 26. Performance Measurement

Track safe article metrics:

- Article views
- Engaged reading
- Scroll depth
- Related article clicks
- Service CTA clicks
- Category clicks
- Search impressions
- Search clicks
- Average position
- Conversion-assisted visits

Do not judge article quality from page views alone.

---

# 27. Content Consolidation

Consider combining articles when:

- Intent overlaps heavily
- One article is thin
- Keywords compete
- Content is outdated
- Two articles answer the same question

Use deliberate redirects and canonical handling.

Do not delete useful articles without a migration plan.
